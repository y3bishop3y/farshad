### BRANCH POINT: How Does Frashad Leave Bombay/the Indian Ocean?

**Path A — He runs (the novel outline):**
He leaves Bombay voluntarily at 18 (1801) after being expelled by the Panchayat. He works dhow routes south and east, building the Fedallah identity over years. His movement is self-directed, driven by the vision he's running from.

**Path B — He is captured (the development notes):**
He is working on a Parsi trading vessel between Bombay and Muscat (~1812–1815) and is captured by Qawasim raiders. Sold into slavery. Passes through Arab slave markets. His identity as Fadl Allah is forced by survival, not chosen.

**Path C — Combination:**
He leaves Bombay voluntarily (expelled, 1801), works the Indian Ocean for a decade as a free man, and is *then* captured by Qawasim raiders (~1812) while working a trade route he thought he knew. The capture is the second exile — first from his community, then from his freedom.

### BRANCH POINT: When and Where Do They Meet?

**Option A (strongest) — Lahaina, ~1830–1833. Frashad becomes Ahab's harpooner BEFORE the leg.**
Both are in the Pacific. Lahaina is where captains and harpooners cross paths. Frashad is already known as "the Parsee who never misses." Ahab — a veteran captain assembling crews for each voyage — recruits Frashad personally. They sail together. Ahab sees through the Fadl Allah performance: *"You pray like a man who learned the words from a book, not from his father."* The thirty years crack. Frashad reveals his real name. They build the bond — harpooner and captain — over one or two voyages before the fateful one.

Then comes the leg voyage (~1836–1839). Frashad is in Ahab's boat when Moby Dick stoves it. Ahab's leg is crushed. Frashad — son of a healer, trained in the knife, the Vendidad's *karetô-baêshaza* — pulls Ahab from the water, applies the tourniquet, and if no surgeon is aboard (many American whalers had none), **performs the amputation himself.** He saves Ahab's life. He creates the cosmic debt.

This is the strongest option because:
- It explains why Ahab trusts Frashad absolutely — they have history, shared voyages, shared danger
- It explains why Ahab says "tell me the prophecy *again*" — Frashad has been telling him things for years
- It explains why Ahab smuggles Frashad aboard the Pequod — the ship's owners (Peleg, Bildad) may blame Frashad for what happened on the leg voyage, or refuse to hire "the Parsee who was there when the captain lost his leg"
- It explains Elijah on the dock — Elijah may have been on the leg voyage too, saw Frashad, saw the bond form, and knows what it means that Ahab brought him back: *"Anything about your souls?"*
- It makes Frashad's death on the Pequod a *return* — he saved Ahab once, interfered with Asha, and now the universe requires him to undo his own act of compassion

**Option B — Nantucket, ~1835:**
Frashad arrives at the center of the whaling world. Ahab is there between voyages. They meet on the docks or in a tavern. Two men, same age, opposite sides of the world. This gives less shared history but a more dramatic first encounter.

**Option C — Indian Ocean, ~1828–1832:**
Ahab sailed everywhere. He could have encountered Frashad on an Indian Ocean voyage — Muscat, Aden, Zanzibar. Two men meeting in the world Frashad came from, before arriving at the world Ahab came from.

*Two men born the same year on opposite sides of the world. One ran from his calling for thirty years. The other created a false calling and followed it to his death. They converged on the same quarterdeck — but they had stood together before, on an earlier ship, when the whale first came. The healer's son cut the captain's leg off to save his life. Then he spent the rest of his life returning the captain to the whale.*

*He died lashed to the Kar-Fish. Pinioned. Turns upon turns. His body joined the guardian he had betrayed for twenty years. His eyes were open. He will circle the Tree of Life forever.*
