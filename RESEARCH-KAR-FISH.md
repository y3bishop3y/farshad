# The Kar-Fish, the Tree of Life, and the Cosmic Ocean — Zoroastrian Mythology

*Research for FRASHAD: A Novel*

---

## The Source: The Bundahishn

The **Bundahishn** ("Creation" or "Knowledge from the Zand") is a Zoroastrian text written in Middle Persian (Pahlavi), compiled from older Avestan sources between the 8th and 12th centuries CE. It is the Zoroastrian creation narrative — their Genesis. It tells how Ahura Mazda (called **Ohrmazd** in the Pahlavi) created the world, how Angra Mainyu (called **Ahriman**) corrupted it, and what must happen before the final renovation (**Frashokereti**) restores everything to perfection.

Chapter 18 describes the cosmic ocean and the creatures that guard the Tree of Life. This is where the Kar-Fish appears.

---

## The Cosmic Ocean: Vourukasha

**Vourukasha** (also Fraxkard, "vast bay") is the world-ocean in Zoroastrian cosmology. It covers roughly a third of the earth's surface. It lies south of the cosmic mountain Hara Berezaiti (Alburz). At its center stands the mountain Ushendava, where vapors gather as rain clouds. Vourukasha is the source of all waters — every river, spring, and rain trace back to it.

This is not a metaphor. In Zoroastrian cosmology, Vourukasha is as real as the Pacific Ocean. It is the primordial sea from which all waters flow and to which all waters return.

---

## The Tree of Life: Gaokerena

In the deepest part of Vourukasha grows the **Gaokerena** — the Tree of Life. The Bundahishn says:

> *"It was the first day when the tree they call Gaokerena grew in the deep mud within the wide-formed ocean; and it is necessary as a producer of the renovation of the universe, for they prepare its immortality therefrom."*

The Gaokerena is not just a tree. It is the instrument of Frashokereti — the final renovation. At the end of time, the Saoshyant (the messianic savior — the word from which Frashad's name derives) will prepare **parahaoma** from the Gaokerena's fruit, and the righteous dead will drink it and become immortal. Without the Gaokerena, there is no resurrection. Without the tree, the dead stay dead.

There is also the **Tree of Many Seeds** (*vispobish*) — growing in the same ocean, containing the seeds of every plant on earth. When a bird called the Chamrosh shakes this tree, its seeds fall into the water and are carried by rain to all the earth's regions. Every plant, every crop, every tree that grows on land originates from seeds that fell from this one tree in the cosmic ocean.

The Gaokerena is also called the **White Haoma** — a shining white tree on which the sacred haoma plant grows. Haoma is the divine plant used in Zoroastrian ritual, associated with healing, alertness, strength, and fertility.

**For the novel:** The Tree of Life grows in the ocean. Its fruit will resurrect the dead. The Saoshyant — the figure Frashad is named for — will prepare it. And the tree is under constant attack.

---

## The Enemy: The Lizard — WHO IS IT?

> *"The evil spirit has formed therein, among those which enter as opponents, a lizard as an opponent in that deep water, so that it may injure the Haoma."*

The lizard is not just *an agent* of Ahriman. **The lizard IS Ahriman.**

Bundahishn Chapter 28 states explicitly: *"His body is that of a lizard (vazagh) whose place is filth."* And Chapter 3 adds: *"The form of the evil spirit was a log-like lizard's (vazak) body."*

**Ahriman — the Zoroastrian devil, the Destructive Spirit, the cosmic enemy of everything good — takes the form of a lizard.** His true body is a lizard's body. The lizard in the deep water trying to destroy the Tree of Life is not a servant of evil. It is evil itself. It is Angra Mainyu in his native form, personally deployed against the single most important object in creation.

The lizard is described as *"the greatest of those proceeding from the evil spirit."* And it sits in the deep water, in the dark, trying to reach the tree that will save the world.

If the lizard succeeds, there is no Gaokerena, no parahaoma, no resurrection, no Frashokereti. The dead stay dead. The universe is never healed. Everything Ahura Mazda created — every person, every prayer, every act of righteousness — was for nothing.

The stakes could not be higher. The entire cosmic plan depends on the Kar-Fish keeping the lizard away from the tree.

### The Lizard as Khrafstra

The lizard belongs to the category of **khrafstra** — noxious creatures created by Ahriman. These include frogs, toads, serpents, scorpions, ants, flies, rats. All are creations of the Evil Spirit. All are enemies of Ahura Mazda's good creation. Killing khrafstra is a sacred duty for Zoroastrians — a meritorious act that can atone for sins.

But the lizard in the deep water is not an ordinary khrafstra. It is the *archetype.* The greatest of Ahriman's creations. The devil in his own shape, sitting at the bottom of the ocean, waiting for the guardians to falter.

### For the Novel

Ahab thinks Moby Dick is evil. He calls the whale "all evil." He projects malice onto it. He believes he is fighting the devil.

But in Frashad's cosmology, Ahab has it exactly backwards. **The whale is the guardian. The lizard is the devil.** The Kar-Fish protects the Tree of Life from destruction. The whale protects cosmic order. When Ahab attacks the whale, he is not fighting evil — he is attacking the creature Ahura Mazda created to *fight* evil.

Ahab is not Ohrmazd striking the lizard. Ahab is the lizard. He is the destructive principle trying to destroy what the guardian protects. He is Angra Mainyu in a Quaker's body, walking on corpse-bone, trying to reach the Tree of Life and burn it down.

And Frashad — the man named for the Saoshyant, the figure who will *use* the tree to resurrect the dead — has been helping the lizard kill the guardians for twenty years.

---

## The Guardians: Ten Kar-Fish

To protect the Tree of Life, Ohrmazd created **ten Kar-Fish**:

> *"Ohrmazd has created there ten Kar fish which, at all times, continually circle around the Haoma, so that the head of one of those fish is continually towards the lizard."*

The Kar-Fish are:

- **The greatest of the creatures of Ohrmazd** — not the greatest fish. The greatest *creature.* Period.
- **Spiritually fed** — they require no physical food. They are sustained by divine will.
- **In eternal contention** with the lizard — they will remain in their protective orbit until Frashokereti, the end of time.
- **"So serpent-like"** that they can detect *"the scratch of a needle's point by which the water shall increase, or by which it is diminishing."* They sense changes in the ocean at a sub-atomic level.
- **Lethal to everything between them** — *"with the jaws of their bodies, moreover, they snap in two whatever of the creatures of both spirits has entered between them."* This is critical: they kill anything — good *or* evil — that enters the space between them and the tree. They are not selective. They are indiscriminate. A creature of Ohrmazd that stumbles between them dies just as fast as a creature of Ahriman. The Kar-Fish do not care who you are. They care where you are. If you are between them and the tree, you die. This is not malice. This is function. They are guardians, not judges.

**For the novel:** When Moby Dick destroys Ahab's whaleboat and takes his leg, it is not personal. The whale — if it is the Kar-Fish — does not hate Ahab. It does not even see Ahab as a person. It sees something that has entered the space between the guardian and what it protects. And it does what Ohrmazd created it to do: *snaps it in two.* Ahab's great error is thinking the whale cares about him. The whale does not. The whale is performing a function older than humanity.

### The Vas of Panchasadvaran

One of the Kar-Fish is singled out for special description:

> *"The Vas of Panchasadvaran... moves within the wide-formed ocean, and its length is as much as what a man, while in a swift race, will walk from dawn till when the sun goes down; so much that it does not itself move the length of the whole of its great body."*

A creature whose body is so vast that it cannot fully move its own length through the water. A day's hard journey from head to tail. Under its special guardianship live all the creatures of the waters.

This is not a fish. This is a whale. This is a leviathan. This is the cosmic prototype of the sperm whale.

---

## The Three-Legged Ass

Standing in the same ocean — Vourukasha — is the **three-legged ass**, one of the most surreal creatures in Zoroastrian mythology:

- Three feet, six eyes, nine mouths, one golden horn with a thousand branches
- Each foot, when placed on the ground, covers an area where a thousand sheep could rest
- Its two ears encompass Mazendaran
- When it dips its neck in the ocean, all the water shakes
- When it cries, all female water creatures become pregnant and all pregnant noxious water creatures miscarry
- **When it urinates in the ocean, all the sea-water is purified** — this is why, the text explains, all asses urinate in water
- **Ambergris is its dung**

That last detail is astonishing for the novel: **ambergris** — the waxy substance found in sperm whale intestines, one of the most valuable products of the whaling industry — is described in the Bundahishn as the dung of the three-legged ass that stands in the cosmic ocean.

Frashad would know this. The substance the whalers prize above almost everything — the ambergris they find in the gut of sperm whales — is, in his mythology, the excrement of a divine creature.

## What Frashad Would Know

A Mobed's son — or a healer's son from an Athornan family — raised in Bombay in the 1790s would have heard these stories. The Bundahishn was transmitted as part of the Zoroastrian oral and priestly tradition. Not every Parsi would know the details, but a boy raised in a household where the Avesta was recited, where the sacred texts were discussed, where the father knew the traditions well enough to be a healer-priest — that boy would know about:

- **Vourukasha** — the cosmic ocean
- **The Gaokerena** — the Tree of Life growing in the deep water, whose fruit will resurrect the dead at Frashokereti
- **The Kar-Fish** — the greatest creatures of Ohrmazd, circling the tree, protecting it from the forces of evil
- **The Vas of Panchasadvaran** — a creature so vast it cannot move its own length
- **The three-legged ass** — the cosmic purifier of the ocean
- **Ambergris** — the dung of the divine ass

## The Devastation — What This Means for the Novel

### Frashad kills the guardians.

Every sperm whale Frashad harpoons is, in the deepest layer of his cosmology, a cousin of the Kar-Fish — the guardians of the Tree of Life, the greatest creatures of Ahura Mazda, the protectors of the instrument of resurrection. He is killing the creatures whose cosmic role is to defend the object that will save all of creation at the end of time.

### The whale is not Angra Mainyu. It is the opposite.

Ahab sees Moby Dick as evil — the embodiment of malice, the "inscrutable thing" he wants to destroy. But in Frashad's cosmology, the whale may be an *instrument of Asha* — cosmic order. The Kar-Fish "snap in two whatever has entered between them." A whaling captain who enters the water to attack them is attacking the guardians. The guardians defend themselves. That is their function.

Moby Dick's destruction of Ahab's boat, the taking of his leg — this is not malice. It is the Kar-Fish doing what Ohrmazd created it to do.

### Frashad is the traitor.

He knows what the whale represents. He hunts it anyway. He hunts it for money. He hunts it because he needs to eat, because he has no other life available, because the ocean is the only place left for him. But the knowledge never leaves. Every time he throws the harpoon, Yasna 29 echoes: *"Slaughter without measure has made my life full of sorrow. Who will protect me?"*

No one. Not Frashad. He is the slaughter now.

### Ambergris — the dung of God's creature

When whalers cut open a sperm whale and find ambergris — the waxy, precious substance worth more per ounce than gold — they celebrate. Frashad stands there knowing that in the Bundahishn, ambergris is the dung of the three-legged ass, the divine creature that purifies the ocean. It is sacred waste. They are selling God's excrement for perfume.

### The Tree of Life is in the ocean. The whale protects it. The whaler destroys the protector.

This is the cosmic geometry of the novel:

- The **Gaokerena** (Tree of Life) grows in the ocean
- The **Kar-Fish** (greatest of all creatures) protect it
- The **Saoshyant** (the figure Frashad is named for) will use its fruit to resurrect the dead
- **Frashad** kills the protectors
- **Ahab** seeks to destroy the greatest of them — Moby Dick — the one that "snaps in two" anything that enters between it and what it guards

Ahab is not fighting God. He is fighting God's bodyguard. And Frashad — the man named for the savior who will use the tree — helps him do it.

Until he doesn't. Until the debt comes due. Until the Kar-Fish takes him first and his corpse, tangled in the line on the whale's body, beckons Ahab into the water.

The guardian reclaims the traitor. Then the guardian takes the captain. The order is restored. Asha.

## The Sacrifice — Frashad Returns Himself to the Kar-Fish

### He Knows What He Is Doing

Frashad's death on Day 2 of the chase is not an accident. It is not bad luck. It is not the random cruelty of a rope catching a man's neck.

**He gives himself to the Kar-Fish.**

He has spent twenty years killing the guardians of the Tree of Life. Twenty years harpooning the creatures Ahura Mazda created to protect the instrument of resurrection. Twenty years serving a man who is, in the deepest layer of Frashad's theology, the lizard — the destructive principle in human form, walking on corpse-bone, trying to destroy the greatest of the Kar-Fish.

And Frashad has accumulated Druj — the Lie, the pollution, the corruption — beyond anything the normal mechanisms of his religion can cleanse:

- The false name (Fadl Allah — thirty years of the Lie)
- The false prayers (facing Mecca, performing salat, living inside Druj to preserve Asha)
- The killing of beneficent creatures (every whale — a cousin of the Kar-Fish, a guardian of the good creation)
- The handling of nasu (Ahab's ivory leg, the whale carcasses, the blubber and bone of creatures that in his theology are sacred)
- The saving of a man the universe was trying to collect (Ahab's leg — the cosmic debt Frashad created by interfering with Asha)
- The service to a man who is functionally Angra Mainyu — the lizard in human form

There is no Barashnum purification ritual at sea. There is no Mobed to perform it. There is no sacred fire to stand before. There is no Tower of Silence where his body can be given to the birds. Every normal avenue of cleansing is closed to him. He is thirty years polluted, on a ship in the middle of the Pacific, with no priest, no fire, no community, no ritual.

Except one.

### The Kar-Fish Is the Purification

The Bundahishn says: *"With the jaws of their bodies, they snap in two whatever of the creatures of both spirits has entered between them."*

The Kar-Fish does not distinguish. It does not judge. It destroys whatever is in its path — good or evil. That destruction is not cruelty. It is *function.* The guardian annihilates everything that enters the space between it and the Tree of Life.

Frashad enters that space.

He puts himself between the whale and the thing it is protecting. He lets the line take him. He lets his body fall onto the whale's back. He becomes part of the guardian — tangled in the rope, lashed to the creature he has spent twenty years killing.

This is the ultimate purification. Not water, not fire, not prayer — but **the jaws of the guardian itself.** The creature that protects the Tree of Life takes Frashad into its orbit, and the corruption is not forgiven. It is not washed away. It is **annihilated.** Destroyed the way the Kar-Fish destroys everything — completely, without remainder, without distinction.

Every sin is burned off by proximity to the guardian. The false name, the false prayers, the whale blood on his hands, the debt he created by saving Ahab — all of it is snapped in two. He is returned to purity not by a priest's ritual but by the oldest mechanism in his cosmology: the guardian consuming whatever stands between it and the tree.

### He Has Merged With the Kar-Fish — Forever

Melville's exact words:

> *"Lashed round and round to the fish's back; pinioned in the turns upon turns in which, during the past night, the whale had reeled the involutions of the lines around him, the half torn body of the Parsee was seen; his sable raiment frayed to shreds; his distended eyes turned full upon old Ahab."*

**Lashed round and round.** Not just tangled. **Pinioned** — bound, fastened, held. The whale had **reeled the involutions of the lines around him** during the night. Turns upon turns upon turns. The rope wound around and around. His body is part of the whale now. Inseparable.

And then: *"as if bent upon escaping with the corpse he bore..."* — the whale is not dragging him. Not scraping him off. The whale is **bearing** him. Carrying him. Swimming forward with the body lashed to its back. The whale has *taken* Frashad.

He has not just died on the whale. He has **joined** it.

In Zoroastrian terms: Frashad's body has merged with the Kar-Fish. He is woven into the guardian by the very ropes that were meant to kill it. The harpoon lines — the instruments of twenty years of slaughter — have become the binding that fuses the traitor to the protector. The weapons of destruction are transformed into the cords of union.

He is lashed to the Kar-Fish forever. His body will go where the whale goes. Into the deep, into the dark, back to the cosmic ocean, back to the Tree of Life. The prodigal son of the guardians has returned — not by standing beside them, but by becoming one of them. His flesh, his bones, his thirty years of Druj — all of it is bound to the creature that guards the Gaokerena until Frashokereti.

He will circle the tree now. With the other Kar-Fish. With his head turned toward the lizard. Forever.

The Zoroastrian funeral rite gives the body to vultures — excarnation. The flesh is consumed so the bones can return to purity. But there are no vultures at sea. There is no Tower of Silence in the Pacific. There is only the whale. And the whale has taken him the way the vultures would have — into its orbit, into its body, into the cycle of the guardian.

A Parsi who dies at sea, with no dakhma, no nasasalar, no ritual — what happens to his body? It belongs to the ocean. And the ocean, in Zoroastrian cosmology, is Vourukasha. And in Vourukasha swim the Kar-Fish. And now Frashad swims with them.

### His Eyes Are Open

Melville: *"his distended eyes turned full upon old Ahab."*

His eyes are open. He is looking at Ahab. The Kar-Fish have "the keenest eyesight of all living beings." And now Frashad's eyes — open, distended, turned full — look out from the whale's back with the same function. He is watching. He is the guardian's eyes now.

He is beckoning.

Not out of malice. Not out of revenge. Out of function. The lizard must be drawn in. The destructive principle must enter the space between the guardian and the tree. And then the guardian does what it was created to do.

Starbuck: *"Moby Dick seeks thee not. It is thou, thou, that madly seekest him!"*

The Kar-Fish does not seek the lizard. The Kar-Fish circles the tree. The lizard comes to the tree. And the Kar-Fish snaps it in two.

Ahab sees the Parsee's open eyes and follows them into the water. The lizard enters the space. The Kar-Fish closes its jaws.

### His Last Word: Asha

When Frashad's last word is *Asha* — truth, cosmic order, righteousness — he is not just saying "mission accomplished." He is saying:

*I am clean.*

*I have returned myself to the guardian I betrayed. The Kar-Fish has taken me. The pollution is gone. The debt is paid. The Tree of Life is defended. I am the sacrifice the lizard cannot be — because I walked in willingly, from inside, carrying thirty years of the Lie, and the guardian burned it all away.*

*I was the prodigal son of the Kar-Fish. I killed my brothers for twenty years. And now I have come home, and the guardian has taken me back the only way it knows how — by snapping me in two with everything else that enters its orbit.*

*The order is restored. The fire is tended. The tree is safe.*

*Asha.*
