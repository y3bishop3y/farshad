◆ Branch Point: How Does Frashad Leave the Indian Ocean?

Path A — He Runs

He leaves Bombay voluntarily at 18 (1801) after being expelled. Works dhow routes for years. Movement is self-directed — driven by the vision he's running from. Takes the name Fedallah as an act of will.

Path B — He Is Captured

Working on a Parsi trading vessel between Bombay and Muscat (~1812–1815), captured by Qawasim raiders. Sold into slavery. Passes through Arab slave markets. The identity of Fadl Allah is forced by survival.

Path C — Combination

Leaves voluntarily (1801), works the Indian Ocean for a decade as a free man, then captured by Qawasim (~1812). The second exile: first from his community, then from his freedom.

◆ Branch Point: When and Where Do They Meet?

Option A (strongest) — Lahaina, ~1830. Frashad becomes Ahab's harpooner BEFORE the leg

"The Parsee who never misses." Ahab recruits Frashad personally. They sail together. Ahab sees through the Fadl Allah performance: "You pray like a man who learned the words from a book, not from his father." Frashad reveals his real name. They build the bond over one or two voyages. Then comes the leg voyage. Frashad is in the boat. He pulls Ahab from the water. He cuts the leg off. He saves the life the universe was trying to end.

Option B — Nantucket, ~1835

Frashad arrives at the center of the whaling world. Ahab is there between voyages. They meet after the leg is already gone. Less shared history but a more dramatic first encounter.

Option C — Indian Ocean, ~1828–32

Ahab sailed everywhere. He could have met Frashad in Muscat, Aden, Zanzibar — the world Frashad came from, before arriving at the world Ahab came from.

The Three Days — The Chase
Jonah: three days in the whale. Jesus: three days in the tomb. Ahab: three days chasing the whale. Three chances. Three refusals.
Day 1
Standing in the bow of Ahab's boat. The mission begins. He has been waiting for this since Lahaina. Since Canton. Since Bombay
Moby Dick surfaces. Boats smashed. Men hurt. First chance to stop. The neighbors knock on the door
Day 2
Frashad dies. Tangled in harpoon lines, dragged under, lashed to the whale's body. The first sacrifice. Not the last
Asha.
The prophet is dead. The cost is real. Second chance to stop. The boat comes. He waves it off
DAY 3
Frashad's corpse surfaces on the whale. Lashed to the white body by harpoon lines. Riding it like a figurehead.

Starbuck sees the whale swimming away: "See! Moby Dick seeks thee not. Not too late to desist!"

The helicopter. The last chance. The universe offering the exit.

Ahab sees the dead Parsee on the whale. The corpse beckons.

He attacks. The whale rams the Pequod. The harpoon line catches Ahab's neck. The ship sinks. Everyone dies. Only Ishmael survives, floating on Queequeg's coffin.

The dead prophet overrules the living. The corpse outranks Starbuck. The prophecy outranks reason. Even dead, Fedallah is still the instrument. His body closes the last door.

The order holds. Asha endures.
