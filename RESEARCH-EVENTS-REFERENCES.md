## Sexual Abuse on Sailing Ships — Documented Cases

### British Royal Navy

- **345 men and boys** prosecuted for "homoerotic crimes" between 1690–1840 (Royal Navy court martial records)
- Sodomy was a **capital offense** from 1627, treated as seriously as mutiny and murder
- Officers used authority to coerce boys — documented in sworn testimony
- The "peg boy" myth (Navy formally supplying boys for sex) is **largely unfounded** — no credible evidence of it as policy. The abuse was real but informal, enabled by power and isolation.
- **Key scholarly source:** *Boys at Sea: Sodomy, Indecency, and Courts Martial in Nelson's Navy* (book)
- **Key scholarly source:** "Officers, Shipboard Boys and Courts Martial for Sodomy and Indecency in the Georgian Navy" — Chapter 5 of *The Social History of English Seamen, 1650–1815* (Cambridge University Press)
- **Key scholarly source:** "Escaping court martial for sodomy: Prosecution and its alternatives in the Royal Navy, 1690–1840" (journal article, SAGE)
- [Royal Museums Greenwich: Sailors and Sexuality](https://www.rmg.co.uk/stories/maritime-history/library-archive/sailors-sexuality-searching-lgbtq-histories-royal-navy)

### American Whaling Ships

**Case 1: The *Montreal* (~1860, New Bedford)**
- Cabin boy **Manuel Enos**, "a youth of tender years and fragile frame"
- Captain **Nathaniel W. Sowle** attempted sodomy soon after sailing
- Enos resisted, escaped in California — Sowle had him **forcibly returned**
- Sowle repeatedly assaulted him using "threats and his physical powers"
- Enos escaped again to a ship sailing to Japan — Sowle had him **returned again** and continued the abuse
- Second cabin boy **Manuel Viera** filed a similar case against the same captain
- Both cases went to the **Hawaii Supreme Court** (*Manuel Enos vs. N. W. Sowle*; *Manuel Viera vs. N. W. Sowle*)
- [Source: gaywhalers.org](https://www.gaywhalers.org/the-cabin-boys)

**Case 2: The *Emily Morgan* (1835)**
- Sailor **John Fryer** caught "trying to have connection with David Stanton in his hindermost parts" — second offense
- Non-consensual advances
- Fryer attempted **suicide** by consuming turpentine and quicksilver
- [Source: The Gay & Lesbian Review](https://glreview.org/article/men-at-sea-in-the-19th-c-the-case-of-fryer/)

**Case 3: The *Joseph Starbuck* (1838–1842)**
- Third Mate **Stephen B. Fisher** sexually harassed and attempted to coerce a boy named **James Chase**
- Documented: unwanted touching, kissing, threats with a razor
- Notably, this ship's name is *Starbuck* — also the name of the Pequod's first mate
- [Source: gaywhalers.org](https://www.gaywhalers.org/the-bad-boys/the-joseph-starbuck)

**Case 4: The *Newport* (1894–1895)**
- Ship log: steward "Scott" was "put forward for Sodomy and Onanism" — received **29 lashes**
- [Source: gaywhalers.org](https://www.gaywhalers.org/background)

**Case 5: The *Charles Phelps* (~1845)**
- Log entry: attempted to "hire a Portuguese deckhand to commit Soddomy"
- [Source: gaywhalers.org](https://www.gaywhalers.org/background)

### Cabin Boy Selection

- Captains chose their own cabin boys — typically young teens
- Some evidence captains occasionally selected "the most visually pleasing and nubile cabin boy for possible use if it arises"
- Boys hired at foreign ports were especially vulnerable — no family connections, no recourse
- Most abuse went undocumented; what survives are the cases that reached courts or appeared in ship logs

## Melville's Own Awareness

Melville sailed on whaling ships between 1841–1844. He knew this world firsthand.

- In **Redburn** (1849): scholars identify "a secret sexual subtext" with "archaic sexual words, sexual puns, symbols, allusions and other coded references to male-male eroticism" — color-coded (red and purple signaling "unspeakable male-male eros")
- In **Moby-Dick** (1851): Ishmael and Queequeg share a bed; scholars identify a chapter as "largely a mutual masturbation pun"; the Ahab-Pip cabin scenes; Fedallah as Ahab's shadow
- In **Billy Budd** (posthumous, 1924): the entire novella is structured around Claggart's obsession with Billy — widely read as repressed homoerotic desire that turns murderous
- Melville wrote "intensely passionate letters" to Nathaniel Hawthorne
- **Key scholarly source:** Jonathan Ned Katz, "Melville's Secret Sex Text: Decoding His 1849 Novel Redburn" ([OutHistory](https://outhistory.org/exhibits/show/melville-s-secret-sex-text--de/mel))
- **Key scholarly source:** "Queering the Dick: Moby-Dick as Coming-Out Narrative" (University of Mary Washington)

## Zoroastrian Heretics and False Prophets — Historical Cases

**Behāfarīd (died 748–49 CE)**
- Zoroastrian from Khorasan who claimed prophetic authority outside the priesthood
- Started a religious peasant revolt blending Zoroastrian and Islamic elements
- Modified prayer practices (7 times daily facing the sun instead of 5), prohibited intoxicants, restricted cattle sacrifice
- **Executed by hanging** — by Abbasid authorities, but with Zoroastrian priestly support
- His followers believed he would return — movement persisted after his death
- Shows: unauthorized prophecy was treated as existential threat, punishable by death, and the Zoroastrian establishment would cooperate with secular authorities to suppress it
- [Source: Iranica Online](https://iranicaonline.org/articles/behafarid-zoroastrian-heresiarch-and-self-styled-prophet-killed-131-748-49)

**The Yatu Tradition**
- **Yatu** = sorcerer in Avestan. Practitioners of **Yatukih** (sorcery).
- Considered servants of Angra Mainyu — agents of Druj
- Described as shape-shifters, nomadic, practitioners of daeva-yasna (demon worship)
- Led by the archetype **Akht-Jadu** ("evil-sorcerer")
- The Zoroastrian attitude toward sorcery is described as **"wholly hostile"** — no grey area, no rehabilitation
- A yatu is not someone who made a mistake. A yatu IS the enemy.

## Things To Research Further

- [ ] **The actual Vendidad text** on sorcery and punishment — what specific penalties are prescribed? Can a yatu be "cured" or is the designation permanent?
- [ ] **Parsee Panchayat records** from the late 18th century — are there documented cases of excommunication for sorcery or heresy (distinct from sexual transgression)?
- [ ] **Zoroastrian dream interpretation tradition** — is there a framework for prophetic dreams? When does vision become sorcery?
- [ ] **The Qissa-i Sanjan** in detail — the epic poem of the Parsee migration. Could be a source of images, language, parallels for Frashad's own exile.
- [ ] **Melville's personal library and reading** — did he read about Zoroastrianism? What sources were available to him in the 1840s? This matters for understanding what Melville *thought* he was writing vs. what the religion actually is.
- [ ] **Dhow routes in detail** — Bombay to Zanzibar to Muscat. How long did passages take? What was life aboard a dhow like? What languages were spoken?
- [ ] **Canton in 1820** — the "factory" system, Parsee opium traders (Jamsetjee Jejeebhoy?), how the Essex story would have circulated
- [ ] **Lahaina in 1830** — daily life in the whaling capital. Where would Fedallah have stayed? How would a Parsee be perceived?
- [ ] **Nantucket in the 1830s** — what did it look like, feel like? Where would Ahab and Fedallah have met? Taverns? The docks? The Quaker community?
